III. Legal Argumentation — National Interest Waiver

To qualify for a National Interest Waiver, the petitioner must demonstrate that the beneficiary meets the three criteria set forth in Matter of Dhanasar, 26 I&N Dec. 884 (AAO 2016). Specifically, the petitioner must show that: (1) the foreign national's proposed endeavor has both substantial merit and national importance; (2) the foreign national is well positioned to advance the proposed endeavor; and (3) on balance, it would be beneficial to the United States to waive the requirements of a job offer and thus of a labor certification.

Dr. Mehta's proposed endeavor is to continue developing AI diagnostic systems for neurodegenerative diseases at the National Institutes of Health, independent of any single employer. This endeavor possesses both substantial merit and national importance, directly addressing critical public health challenges facing the United States.

Substantial Merit: Dr. Mehta's work focuses on an area of profound scientific and medical complexity: the early and accurate diagnosis of neurodegenerative diseases, such as Alzheimer's. His research involves the application of advanced Artificial Intelligence and machine learning techniques to analyze complex medical data, including imaging and clinical markers, to identify disease patterns that are often imperceptible to the human eye in early stages.

National Importance: Neurodegenerative diseases, particularly Alzheimer's, represent a growing public health crisis in the United States. The CDC projects that by 2060, the number of people aged 65 and older with Alzheimer's could nearly triple, from 6.2 million to 14 million. Dr. Mehta's AI algorithm for early Alzheimer detection has already been successfully deployed in 8 U.S. hospitals, resulting in:

  • Economic benefits — mitigating the hundreds of billions in annual costs associated with Alzheimer's and other dementias
  • Advancement of medical science — positioning the U.S. at the forefront of AI-driven healthcare innovation
