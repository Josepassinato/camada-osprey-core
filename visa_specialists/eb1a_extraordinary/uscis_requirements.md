# EB-1A Extraordinary Ability - USCIS Requirements

## Overview
EB-1A is for individuals with extraordinary ability in sciences, arts, education, business, or athletics. This is the highest employment-based preference category and allows self-petition without employer sponsorship.

## Basic Requirements

### Must demonstrate:
- **Sustained national or international acclaim**
- **Recognition** as being at the very top of the field
- That you are among the **small percentage** who have risen to the very top

### Evidence Options:

**EITHER:**
1. Evidence of a **one-time achievement** (major internationally-recognized award)
   - Nobel Prize
   - Oscar, Emmy, Grammy
   - Olympic Medal  
   - Pulitzer Prize
   - Similar major international award

**OR:**

2. Evidence meeting **at least 3 of the following 10 criteria:**

## The 10 Criteria

### 1. Awards and Prizes
- Receipt of lesser nationally or internationally recognized prizes or awards for excellence
- Must be nationally or internationally recognized
- Should be prestigious in your field

### 2. Membership in Associations
- Membership in associations in the field
- Membership must require outstanding achievements
- Judged by recognized national or international experts

### 3. Published Material About You
- Published material about you in professional or major trade publications or major media
- About you and your work
- In professional journals or major media

### 4. Judging Others' Work
- Participation, individually or on a panel, as a judge of the work of others
- In same or allied field of specialization
- As peer reviewer, grant reviewer, competition judge, etc.

### 5. Original Contributions
- Original scientific, scholarly, artistic, athletic, or business-related contributions
- Of major significance to the field
- Must show impact and recognition

### 6. Scholarly Articles
- Authorship of scholarly articles in professional journals or major media
- Should show significant impact/citations
- Quality over quantity

### 7. Artistic Exhibitions or Showcases
- Display of work at artistic exhibitions or showcases
- At distinguished venues
- National or international scope

### 8. Leading or Critical Role
- Leading or critical role for organizations with distinguished reputation
- Must show your role was significant
- Organization must have distinguished reputation

### 9. High Salary/Remuneration
- High salary or other significantly high remuneration
- In relation to others in your field
- Must provide comparative data

### 10. Commercial Success in Performing Arts
- Commercial successes in performing arts
- Box office receipts, record sales, etc.
- Industry recognition

## Required Documents

1. **Form I-140** - Petition for Alien Worker

2. **Evidence for 3+ Criteria** (or one-time major award)
   - Detailed documentation for each criterion claimed

3. **Letters of Recommendation** (8-10 recommended)
   - From recognized experts in your field
   - From various countries/institutions
   - Attesting to your extraordinary ability and sustained acclaim

4. **Comprehensive CV/Resume**

5. **Personal Statement**
   - Your achievements and contributions
   - Your plans for continuing work in U.S.
   - Why your work benefits the U.S.

## Filing Fee
- **$700** (Form I-140)
- Premium Processing available: $2,500 for 45-day processing

## Processing Time
- Regular: 8-12 months
- Premium: 45 days (if available)

## Advantages
- Highest employment-based preference (shortest wait times)
- No job offer required
- No labor certification required
- Self-petition (no employer sponsor needed)
- Can include spouse and children under 21
- Faster green card processing

## Standard of Proof
- Preponderance of evidence
- Must show you are in small percentage at very top of field
- Recent achievements weighted more heavily
- Quality more important than quantity

## Common Mistakes
- Claiming too many criteria weakly instead of 3-4 strongly
- Generic recommendation letters
- Insufficient evidence of national/international recognition
- Not demonstrating sustained acclaim
- Not showing you're at the very top of field
- Awards/recognitions that aren't nationally/internationally recognized
