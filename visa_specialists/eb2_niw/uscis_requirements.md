# EB-2 NIW (National Interest Waiver) - USCIS Requirements

## Overview
EB-2 NIW allows foreign nationals with advanced degrees or exceptional ability to self-petition for a green card by demonstrating their work is in the national interest of the United States, waiving the job offer and labor certification requirements.

## Basic Requirements

### Education/Experience:
Must meet EB-2 requirements:
- **Advanced Degree**: Master's degree or higher (or Bachelor's + 5 years progressive experience)
- **OR Exceptional Ability**: Significantly above ordinary in sciences, arts, or business

### Three-Prong Test (Matter of Dhanasar, 2016):

Must demonstrate:

1. **Substantial Merit and National Importance**
   - Work has substantial intrinsic merit
   - Proposed endeavor has national (not just local) importance
   - Can be in any field (STEM, business, education, arts, entrepreneurship, etc.)

2. **Well Positioned to Advance the Endeavor**
   - Education, skills, knowledge, track record
   - Plan for continuing work
   - Progress toward achieving the proposed endeavor
   - Interest from potential customers, users, investors, or relevant entities

3. **Beneficial to Waive Requirements**
   - On balance, would be beneficial to U.S. to waive job offer/labor cert
   - Demonstrate urgency, impracticality of obtaining labor certification
   - Significant prospective benefit to U.S.

## Required Documents

1. **Form I-140** - Petition for Alien Worker

2. **Educational Credentials**
   - Diplomas, degrees, transcripts
   - Credential evaluation (if degree from outside U.S.)

3. **Evidence of Exceptional Ability/Achievement**
   - Publications in professional journals
   - Citations to your work
   - Patents
   - Evidence of significant contributions to field
   - Awards and recognition
   - Professional memberships
   - High salary or remuneration

4. **Letters of Recommendation**
   - 5-8 letters from independent experts
   - Should address your qualifications and national interest
   - From U.S. and international experts

5. **Personal Statement**
   - Detailed explanation of proposed endeavor
   - How it meets Dhanasar criteria
   - Your qualifications and plans

6. **Evidence of National Interest**
   - Impact of your work
   - How it benefits U.S.
   - Urgency/importance of your work
   - Why waiver is justified

## Filing Fee
- **$700** (Form I-140)
- **$85** (Biometrics, if required)

## Processing Time
- Regular: 12-18 months
- Premium Processing (additional $2,500): 45 days (if available)

## After Approval
- File I-485 (Adjustment of Status) if in U.S.
- OR Consular Processing if outside U.S.
- Can include spouse and children under 21

## Advantages of NIW
- No job offer required
- No labor certification (PERM) required
- Self-petition (don't need employer sponsor)
- Can change jobs after I-485 filed 180+ days
- Concurrent I-485 filing possible

## Common Mistakes
- Weak demonstration of national importance
- Insufficient evidence of qualifications
- Generic recommendation letters
- Not addressing all three Dhanasar prongs
- Insufficient citation/impact evidence
