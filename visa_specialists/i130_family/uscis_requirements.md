# I-130 Petition for Alien Relative - USCIS Requirements

## Overview
Form I-130 is used by U.S. citizens and lawful permanent residents to establish a relationship to certain foreign-born relatives who wish to immigrate to the United States.

## Eligible Relationships

### U.S. Citizens can petition for:
- Spouse
- Unmarried children under 21
- Unmarried children over 21
- Married children of any age
- Brothers and sisters (if petitioner is over 21)
- Parents (if petitioner is over 21)

### Lawful Permanent Residents can petition for:
- Spouse
- Unmarried children of any age

## Required Documents

1. **Form I-130** - Completed and signed
2. **Proof of Petitioner's Status**
   - U.S. birth certificate, passport, or naturalization certificate
   - OR Green Card (front and back)

3. **Proof of Relationship**
   - **For Spouse**: Marriage certificate, proof of termination of prior marriages
   - **For Parent/Child**: Birth certificate showing relationship
   - **For Siblings**: Birth certificates showing common parent(s)

4. **Identity Documents**
   - Passport-style photos (2 for each person)
   - Copies of passports

5. **Evidence of Bona Fide Relationship** (especially for spouses)
   - Joint bank accounts
   - Joint lease or mortgage
   - Joint utility bills
   - Joint tax returns
   - Birth certificates of children together
   - Photos together
   - Affidavits from friends/family

## Filing Fee
- **$535** (as of 2024)

## Processing Time
- Immediate relatives: 10-24 months
- Other family preferences: 1-10+ years depending on preference category

## Important Notes
- Priority date is established when petition is filed
- Approval of I-130 does not grant immigration status
- Additional forms (DS-260 or I-485) required for actual immigration
- Must prove relationship is genuine, not entered for immigration fraud
