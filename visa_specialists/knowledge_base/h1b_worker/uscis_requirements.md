# H-1B Specialty Occupation Worker - USCIS Knowledge Base

## Fonte
- USCIS Form I-129 Instructions
- USCIS Policy Manual - Volume 2: Nonimmigrants - Part H

- Última atualização: 2025

---

## 1. PROPÓSITO DO H-1B

### 1.1 O Que É
- **H-1B:** Nonimmigrant visa para specialty occupations
- **Propósito:** Permitir employers contratar foreign workers em profissões especializadas
- **Employer-sponsored:** Empregador petitions para o employee

### 1.2 Specialty Occupation Definition
✅ **REQUISITOS:**
- Requires theoretical and practical application of specialized knowledge
- Requires bachelor's degree or higher (ou equivalent)
- Degree requirement is common in the industry
- Position duties are so specialized/complex que requer degree

**Exemplos:** Software Engineer, Architect, Accountant, Analyst, Engineer

---

## 2. ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS

### 2.1 Para o Beneficiary (Worker)
✅ **DEVE TER:**
- Bachelor's degree ou higher em specialty field
- OU equivalent experience (3 anos work = 1 ano college)
- Degree relacionado ao position
- Qualificações para perform duties

### 2.2 Para o Employer (Petitioner)
✅ **DEVE DEMONSTRAR:**
- Legitimate business nos EUA
- Position qualifies como specialty occupation
- Ability to pay prevailing wage
- Approved Labor Condition Application (LCA)
- Will comply with attestations

### 2.3 Position Requirements
✅ **SPECIALTY OCCUPATION MUST:**
- Require bachelor's degree minimum
- Be related to specialized field
- Have duties que require specialized knowledge
- Pay prevailing wage ou actual wage (whichever higher)

---

## 3. H-1B CAP E LOTTERY

### 3.1 Annual Cap
📊 **LIMITES:**
- **Regular Cap:** 65,000 visas/ano
- **Master's Cap:** 20,000 visas adicionais (US master's degree)
- **Total:** 85,000 visas/ano

### 3.2 Cap-Exempt Employers
✅ **SEM CAP LIMIT:**
- Higher education institutions
- Non-profit research organizations
- Government research organizations
- Non-profit entities affiliated com university

### 3.3 Lottery System
⚠️ **COMO FUNCIONA:**
- Mais applicants que visas = lottery
- Registration period: March (geralmente)
- Selected cases: Can file full petition
- Not selected: Wait next fiscal year

### 3.4 Start Dates
- **Fiscal Year:** October 1st
- **Registration:** March (typic)
- **Filing period:** April 1 - June 30
- **Start work:** October 1 (earliest)

---

## 4. LABOR CONDITION APPLICATION (LCA)

### 4.1 O Que É LCA
- **Submitted to:** Department of Labor (DOL)
- **ANTES:** de filing I-129 with USCIS
- **Propósito:** Attest working conditions
- **Form:** ETA-9035/9035E

### 4.2 LCA Attestations
✅ **EMPLOYER ATTESTS:**
1. Will pay prevailing wage ou actual wage (higher)
2. Working conditions won't adversely affect US workers
3. No strike/lockout at worksite
4. Notice provided to employees/union

### 4.3 Prevailing Wage
💰 **DETERMINATION:**
- Based on occupation, location, experience level
- Sources: DOL wage data, OES survey, employer survey
- Must meet or exceed prevailing wage
- Wage verificado at time of LCA filing

### 4.4 LCA Processing
⏱️ **TIMING:**
- Filed online via FLAG system
- Typically certified within 7 days
- Valid for filing date + 6 months
- Valid for employment period up to 3 years

## 5. FORM I-129 E DOCUMENTAÇÃO

### 5.1 Formulários Necessários
✅ **OBRIGATÓRIOS:**
- **Form I-129** (Petition for Nonimmigrant Worker)
- **H Classification Supplement** (page specific to H-1B)
- **H-1B Data Collection Supplement** (fee info)
- **LCA (certified)** - ORIGINAL
- Filing fee

### 5.2 Filing Fees
💰 **VALORES:**
- **Base fee (I-129):** $460
- **Fraud Prevention fee:** $500 (first H-1B for beneficiary by employer)
- **ACWIA Training fee:** $750 (employers <25 employees) ou $1,500 (25+ employees)
- **Premium Processing (optional):** $2,500 (15-day processing)
- **Total típico:** $2,460 a $2,710 (sem premium)

### 5.3 Fee Exemptions
✅ **ACWIA EXEMPT:**
- Higher education institutions
- Non-profit research organizations
- Government research organizations
- Extensions/amendments (not new employment)
- Certain cap-exempt employers

---

## 6. DOCUMENTAÇÃO OBRIGATÓRIA

### 6.1 Employer Documents
✅ **CRITICAL:**
- **LCA (certified original)** - MUST HAVE
- **Job offer letter** detailing position, duties, salary
- **Employer evidence:** Articles of incorporation, business license
- **Financial documents:** Tax returns, financial statements
- **Organizational chart** (se grande company)
- **Previous H-1Bs:** Evidence se já sponsorou outros

### 6.2 Position Documents
✅ **PROVE SPECIALTY OCCUPATION:**
- Detailed job description com specific duties
- Evidence position requires degree:
  - Industry standards
  - Job postings requiring degree
  - Expert opinion letter
  - Employer's past practice
- Explanation how degree relates to duties

### 6.3 Beneficiary Documents
✅ **QUALIFICATIONS:**
- **Degree:** Diploma, transcripts (com avaliação se foreign)
- **Resume/CV**
- **Employment letters:** Previous positions, experience
- **Licenses/Certifications** (se required)
- **Published works** (se relevant)

### 6.4 Wage Documents
✅ **PREVAILING WAGE:**
- LCA com prevailing wage determination
- Offer letter stating wage
- Evidence employer can pay wage

---

## 7. CREDENTIAL EVALUATION (Foreign Degrees)

### 7.1 Quando Necessário
- Degree earned outside USA
- USCIS precisa verificar equivalency

### 7.2 Evaluation Requirements
✅ **DEVE INCLUIR:**
- Official evaluation de NACES ou similar
- Statement que degree = US bachelor's ou higher
- Explanation of equivalency
- Evaluator credentials

### 7.3 Three-for-One Rule
- **3 anos** work experience = **1 ano** college credit
- Can combine education + experience
- Must be progressive, specialized experience

---

## 8. TIMING E VALIDITY

### 8.1 Initial Period
- Maximum: **3 anos**
- Can request shorter period
- Must match LCA validity period

### 8.2 Extensions
- Can extend in **3-year increments**
- **Maximum total:** 6 years
- Exceptions exist for pending green card cases

### 8.3 AC21 Extensions (beyond 6 years)
✅ **IF:**
- I-140 approved + waiting for visa number
- OU I-140 pending for 365+ days
- Can extend H-1B in 1 ou 3 year increments

### 8.4 Processing Time
⏱️ **STANDARD:**
- 2-6 meses (varia by service center)

⏱️ **PREMIUM PROCESSING:**
- 15 calendar days
- Fee: $2,500
- USCIS must respond within 15 days or refund fee

---

## 9. PORTABILITY (AC21 §105)

### 9.1 Starting New Job
✅ **CAN START WORK:**
- Quando new H-1B petition filed (receipt notice)
- IF beneficiary is:
  - In valid H-1B status, OR
  - In grace period after H-1B employment end

### 9.2 Requirements
- New petition filed before previous expires
- Previous petition was approved
- New position also qualifies for H-1B

---

## 10. H-4 DEPENDENTS

### 10.1 Eligibilidade
✅ **PODE OBTER H-4:**
- Spouse of H-1B
- Unmarried children under 21

### 10.2 H-4 Benefits
✅ **PODE:**
- Live in USA with H-1B
- Study (any level)
- Apply for driver's license
- Travel in/out of USA

❌ **NÃO PODE:**
- Work (unless eligible for H-4 EAD)

### 10.3 H-4 EAD
✅ **ELEGIBLE SE:**
- H-1B has approved I-140, OR
- H-1B in AC21 extension status (6+ years)
- File Form I-765

---

## 11. CHANGE OF EMPLOYER

### 11.1 Process
1. New employer files new I-129
2. Include copy of previous H-1B approval
3. Can start work when new petition filed (portability)
4. Must file before previous expires

### 11.2 Documents Needed
- All standard H-1B documents
- Copy of previous H-1B approval notice
- Copy of current I-94
- Explanation of change

---

## 12. AMENDMENTS E EXTENSIONS

### 12.1 Amendment (quando necessário)
⚠️ **FILE AMENDMENT IF:**
- Material change in employment:
  - New position
  - New location (não on LCA)
  - Significant duties change
  - Salary change (decrease)

### 12.2 Extension
✅ **FILE BEFORE EXPIRATION:**
- 6 meses antes recomendado
- Minimum 2-3 meses antes
- Include all original documents + updates

---

## 13. MAINTAINING H-1B STATUS

### 13.1 Requirements
✅ **MUST:**
- Work only for petitioning employer
- Work only in approved position/location
- Maintain valid H-1B status
- Depart if status ends

### 13.2 Benched/Unpaid Leave
⚠️ **WARNING:**
- H-1B must be paid even when no work
- "Benching" without pay = status violation
- Employer must pay agreed wage

### 13.3 Violations
❌ **RESULT IN STATUS LOSS:**
- Unauthorized employment
- Working for non-petitioning employer
- Extended unpaid leave
- Termination without departure

---

## 14. TRAVEL

### 14.1 Traveling Outside USA
✅ **NEED:**
- Valid H-1B visa stamp (if returning)
- Valid H-1B approval notice (I-797)
- Valid passport
- Employment letter

### 14.2 Visa Stamping
- Done at US consulate abroad
- Required for re-entry
- Visa stamp can expire while in USA (OK)
- Must be valid for re-entry

### 14.3 Automatic Revalidation
- For trips to Canada/Mexico <30 days
- Don't need new visa stamp
- Must maintain valid status

## 15. ARMADILHAS COMUNS

### 15.1 ❌ ERRO FATAL #1: LCA Issues
**Problema:** LCA incorreta, expirada, ou missing
**Consequência:** RFE ou negação
**Solução:** Ensure LCA certified, valid, matches I-129

### 15.2 ❌ ERRO FATAL #2: Não Provar Specialty Occupation
**Problema:** Insufficient evidence position requires degree
**Consequência:** Negação
**Solução:** Detailed job description, industry standards, expert letters

### 15.3 ❌ ERRO FATAL #3: Beneficiary Não Qualificado
**Problema:** Degree não relacionado, insufficient experience
**Consequência:** Negação
**Solução:** Foreign evaluation, equivalency explanation, experience letters

### 15.4 ❌ ERRO FATAL #4: Prevailing Wage Incorreto
**Problema:** Offered wage below prevailing
**Consequência:** LCA denied, I-129 denied
**Solução:** Verify wage, use correct SOC code, proper location

### 15.5 ❌ ERRO FATAL #5: Missing Documents
**Problema:** Incomplete petition
**Consequência:** RFE ou rejection
**Solução:** Complete checklist, all required evidence

---

## 16. RAZÕES COMUNS DE NEGAÇÃO

### 16.1 Top Denial Reasons
1. **Position Não É Specialty Occupation (40%)**
   - Duties too general
   - Degree not normally required
   - Industry standards not met

2. **Beneficiary Não Qualificado (25%)**
   - Degree não relacionado
   - Insufficient experience
   - Credentials questionable

3. **Employer Credibility (15%)**
   - Unable to verify business
   - Financial inability to pay
   - Past compliance issues

4. **LCA Issues (10%)**
   - Wage issues
   - Location issues
   - Expired LCA

5. **Documentation Issues (10%)**
   - Missing documents
   - Inconsistencies
   - Poor quality evidence

## 17. PATH TO GREEN CARD

### 17.1 Employment-Based Green Card
- H-1B can pursue permanent residency
- Employer sponsors I-140
- Then I-485 (if visa available)

### 17.2 Dual Intent
- H-1B allows dual intent
- Can pursue green card while on H-1B
- Won't affect H-1B renewals
