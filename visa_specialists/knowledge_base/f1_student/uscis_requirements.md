# F-1 Student Visa - USCIS Policy Manual Knowledge Base

## Fonte
USCIS Policy Manual - Volume 2: Nonimmigrants
8 CFR § 214.2(f) - Student visas
Última atualização: 2025

---

## 1. REQUISITOS DE ELEGIBILIDADE

### 1.1 Quem Pode Aplicar
- Estudantes aceitos em instituição aprovada pelo SEVP (Student and Exchange Visitor Program)
- Programa acadêmico ou language training
- Full-time student status (mínimo 12 credit hours por semester para graduação)
- Proficiência em inglês suficiente (ou ESL enrollment)

### 1.2 Condições de Elegibilidade
✅ **DEVE TER:**
- Form I-20 emitido por escola SEVP-certified
- Acceptance letter da instituição
- Prova de proficiência em inglês (TOEFL, IELTS, etc.) OU enrollment em ESL
- Recursos financeiros para período completo de estudos
- Vínculos com país de origem
- Intenção de retornar após conclusão dos estudos
- Residência no exterior que não pretende abandonar

❌ **NÃO PODE:**
- Ter immigrant intent
- Planejar trabalhar illegalmente
- Insuficientes recursos financeiros
- Histórico de violações de status

---

## 2. FORM I-20 (Certificate of Eligibility)

### 2.1 O que é I-20
- Documento oficial emitido pela escola SEVP-certified
- Prova de acceptance
- Contém SEVIS ID number
- Necessário para visa application e entry

### 2.2 Informações no I-20
✅ **INCLUI:**
- Student information
- Program details (degree, field of study, length)
- Program start and end dates
- Cost of attendance (tuition + living expenses)
- Financial support sources
- SEVIS ID number
- School DSO signature

### 2.3 Maintaining Valid I-20
⚠️ **CRITICAL:**
- Must maintain full-time enrollment
- Report address changes within 10 days
- Transfer to new school requires I-20 transfer
- Program extensions require new I-20
- OPT/CPT requires updated I-20

---

## 3. SEVIS (Student and Exchange Visitor Information System)

### 3.1 SEVIS Fee
- **Amount:** $350
- **When:** Antes de aplicar para visto
- **Valid:** 12 meses ou until program start
- **Receipt:** Form I-797 - guardar para entrevista

### 3.2 SEVIS Status
- Active: Full-time enrollment, compliance
- Terminated: Violations, unauthorized employment, etc.
- Must maintain active SEVIS status sempre

---

## 4. DOCUMENTAÇÃO OBRIGATÓRIA

### 4.1 Para Visa Application (Consular Processing)
✅ **OBRIGATÓRIOS:**
- Form DS-160 (Online Nonimmigrant Visa Application)
- Valid passport (6+ months beyond program end)
- Form I-20 from SEVP school
- SEVIS fee receipt (I-797)
- Visa application fee ($185)
- Passport photo (2x2 inches)
- Academic documents:
  - School transcripts
  - Diplomas/certificates
  - Standardized test scores (SAT, GRE, GMAT if applicable)
  - English proficiency test (TOEFL/IELTS)

### 4.2 Financial Evidence ⭐ CRÍTICO
✅ **OBRIGATÓRIOS:**
- Bank statements (últimos 6-12 meses)
- Scholarship letters
- Sponsor's financial documents
- Affidavit of Support with sponsor's:
  - Bank statements
  - Tax returns (3 years)
  - Employment letter
- Loan approval letters

💰 **VALORES:**
- Must cover: Tuition + Living Expenses
- Typical: $20,000-$70,000 per year depending on school
- Show amount for ENTIRE program duration

### 4.3 Ties to Home Country
✅ **DOCUMENTOS:**
- Property ownership
- Family ties (parents, siblings in home country)
- Employment prospects after graduation
- Assets in home country
- Community involvement

---

## 5. DOCUMENTOS PROIBIDOS/NÃO PERMITIDOS

### 5.1 ❌ NUNCA INCLUIR

**Trabalho:**
- ❌ Job offers (except CPT/OPT authorized)
- ❌ Employment contracts
- ❌ Work authorization (except I-765 for OPT)

**Imigração Permanente:**
- ❌ I-140, I-485
- ❌ Green card applications
- ❌ Documents showing immigrant intent

**Outros Vistos:**
- ❌ B-1/B-2 applications
- ❌ H-1B petitions (unless after graduation with proper timing)

---

## 6. WORKING ON F-1 STATUS

### 6.1 On-Campus Employment
✅ **PERMITTED:**
- Maximum 20 hours/week during school
- Full-time during breaks
- No authorization needed
- Start after first semester (some exceptions)

### 6.2 CPT (Curricular Practical Training)
✅ **REQUIREMENTS:**
- After 1 year of F-1 status
- Integral part of curriculum
- Must be related to major
- Authorized on I-20 by DSO
- Part-time or full-time

⚠️ **WARNING:**
- 12+ months full-time CPT = NO OPT eligibility

### 6.3 OPT (Optional Practical Training)
✅ **REQUIREMENTS:**
- After 1 year of F-1 status
- Related to field of study
- Form I-765 (Application for Employment Authorization)
- Fee: $410 + $85
- Processing time: 3-5 months

**Types:**
1. **Pre-Completion OPT:** Before graduation (part-time or full-time during breaks)
2. **Post-Completion OPT:** After graduation (12 months)
3. **STEM OPT Extension:** Additional 24 months for STEM degrees

### 6.4 STEM OPT Extension
✅ **ELIGIBILITY:**
- STEM degree (Science, Technology, Engineering, Math)
- E-Verify employer
- Additional 24 months after initial 12-month OPT
- Form I-983 (Training Plan)

## 7. MAINTAINING F-1 STATUS

### 7.1 ✅ MUST DO:
1. Maintain full-time enrollment (12+ credits/semester)
2. Make normal academic progress
3. Keep valid passport
4. Keep current I-20
5. Report address changes within 10 days
6. Don't work without authorization
7. Don't overstay
8. Maintain SEVIS active status

### 7.2 ❌ VIOLATIONS (Result in Status Termination):
- Drop below full-time enrollment (without DSO approval)
- Unauthorized employment
- Overstay (after grace period)
- Transfer without I-20 transfer
- Criminal violations

### 7.3 Grace Periods
- **Before Program:** 30 days before I-20 start date
- **After Completion:** 60 days after completion or OPT end
- **Transfer:** 15-day grace period for school transfer

---

## 8. PROGRAM CHANGES

### 8.1 Extension of Program
- Contact DSO before I-20 end date
- Request I-20 program extension
- Valid reason: Academic delays, change of major, etc.
- No USCIS filing needed if timely

### 8.2 School Transfer
✅ **PROCESS:**
1. Accept new school
2. Request transfer-in I-20 from new school
3. Complete transfer in SEVIS within 15 days of program end
4. Report to new DSO
5. Transfer must complete within 5 months

### 8.3 Change of Level
- Undergraduate to graduate OK
- Must get new I-20
- May need brief return to home country if changing from low-level to high-level program

---

## 9. ARMADILHAS COMUNS

### 9.1 ❌ ERRO FATAL #1: Unauthorized Work
**Problema:** Working off-campus without authorization
**Consequência:** Status termination, removal proceedings
**Solução:** Only work with proper authorization (on-campus, CPT, OPT)

### 9.2 ❌ ERRO FATAL #2: Dropped Courses
**Problema:** Falling below 12 credits without DSO approval
**Consequência:** Status violation, SEVIS termination
**Solução:** Always consult DSO BEFORE dropping courses

### 9.3 ❌ ERRO FATAL #3: Late OPT Application
**Problema:** Applying for OPT too late
**Consequência:** Missing work opportunity, gap in authorization
**Solução:** Apply 90 days before graduation, no later than 60 days after

### 9.4 ❌ ERRO FATAL #4: Not Reporting Address
**Problema:** Moving without updating SEVIS
**Consequência:** Status termination
**Solução:** Report within 10 days of moving

### 9.5 ❌ ERRO FATAL #5: Expired Passport
**Problema:** Let passport expire while in F-1 status
**Consequência:** Cannot maintain valid status, cannot travel
**Solução:** Renew passport well before expiration

## 10. CHANGE OF STATUS TO F-1

### 10.1 Form I-539 (If Already in USA)
✅ **REQUIREMENTS:**
- Currently in valid nonimmigrant status
- Form I-539 + I-20
- Fee: $370 + $85
- Filed BEFORE current status expires
- Demonstrate nonimmigrant intent

⚠️ **LIMITATIONS:**
- Cannot work while I-539 pending
- Cannot travel while I-539 pending
- If denied, must leave immediately

---

## 11. DEPENDENTS (F-2 Status)

### 11.1 Who Qualifies
- Spouse
- Unmarried children under 21

### 11.2 F-2 Restrictions
✅ **CAN:**
- Study part-time or recreational
- Study full-time in elementary/secondary school

❌ **CANNOT:**
- Work (no employment authorization)
- Study full-time in college (must change to F-1)

## 12. INTERVIEW TIPS (Consular)

### 12.1 Common Questions
1. Why this school/program?
2. How will you pay for studies?
3. What will you do after graduation?
4. Why USA and not home country?
5. Ties to home country?
6. Future career plans?

### 12.2 ✅ Good Answers
- Specific about program/school
- Clear funding sources with documentation
- Strong ties to home country
- Intent to return after studies
- Career plans that benefit home country

### 12.3 ❌ Bad Answers (Red Flags)
- "I want to work in USA" (immigrant intent)
- Vague about funding
- No clear post-graduation plans
- Weak ties to home country
- Previous visa violations

---

## 13. DENIAL REASONS

### 13.1 Common Denial Reasons
1. **Immigrant Intent (INA §214(b))** - Most common
   - Weak ties to home country
   - Unclear plans to return
   
2. **Insufficient Funds (Public Charge)**
   - Cannot demonstrate financial ability
   - Unclear funding sources

3. **Not Qualified Student**
   - Low English proficiency
   - Poor academic record
   - Inconsistent education history

4. **Previous Violations**
   - Overstays
   - Unauthorized work
   - False documents

---

## 14. POST-GRADUATION OPTIONS

### 14.1 Options After F-1
1. **Return Home** (60-day grace period)
2. **Continue Studies** (Transfer to another program)
3. **OPT** (Work authorization for 12-36 months)
4. **Change Status:**
   - H-1B (requires employer sponsorship)
   - O-1 (extraordinary ability)
   - Marriage to US citizen/LPR
5. **Leave and Re-enter** (if pursuing another degree)
