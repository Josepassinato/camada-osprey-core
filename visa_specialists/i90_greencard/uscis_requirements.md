# I-90 Application to Replace Permanent Resident Card - USCIS Requirements

## Overview
Form I-90 is used to renew or replace a Permanent Resident Card (Green Card).

## Reasons to File I-90

1. **Card Expiring or Expired**
   - File 6 months before expiration
   - 10-year card standard validity

2. **Lost or Stolen Card**
   - File immediately
   - Consider filing police report

3. **Damaged or Destroyed Card**
   - Card must be unreadable or damaged

4. **Name Change**
   - Legal name change through marriage, divorce, or court order

5. **Information Error**
   - USCIS error on the card

6. **Card Issued Before 14th Birthday**
   - Must replace within 30 days of 14th birthday

7. **Card Never Received**
   - Original card lost in mail

## Required Documents

1. **Form I-90** - Completed and signed
2. **Two passport-style photos**
3. **Copy of current/expired Green Card** (front and back)
4. **Copy of passport** (biographical page)

### Additional Documents by Reason:

#### For Name Change:
- Marriage certificate, divorce decree, or court order

#### For Lost/Stolen Card:
- Police report (recommended)
- Affidavit explaining circumstances

#### For Error Correction:
- Evidence of correct information

## Filing Fee
- **$455** (form fee)
- **$85** (biometrics fee)
- **Total: $540**

## Processing Time
- Generally 8-12 months
- Automatic extension with receipt notice

## Important Notes
- Can still travel with expired card + receipt notice (within 1 year)
- Must maintain permanent resident status
- Cannot file if in removal proceedings
- File online or by mail
- Keep copy of receipt notice for your records

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Filing too late (file 6 months before expiration)
- Not including all required documents
- Incorrect photos (must meet USCIS specifications)
- Wrong filing fee
